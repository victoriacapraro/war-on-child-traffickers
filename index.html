The "war on child traffickers" isn't as big as you think it is.

The statistics about how many children in the U.S. go missing every year are doctored and inaccurate. And as the far-right rallies around #SavetheChildren, that's a big problem.

By Victoria Capraro

From the Epstein scandal, to the Wayfair conspiracy, to "Pizzagate," misinformation has spread about human trafficking in the U.S. this year. Here is a look into accuracy some of the most popular statistics on the internet right now:

This meme is widely circulated on the #SaveTheChildren and other trafficking hashtags. It claims that 800,000 children in the U.S. disappear each year. If this were true, then about 7% of all children in the U.S. would disappear annually.

The number comes from a 2002 Department of Justice study that looked at missing children reports dating from 1999. About 115 of the 800,000 reported were victims of kidnapping by a stranger or distant contact. Most of the incidents were considered runaways or instances of caregiver miscommunication. Think: one parent in a custody battle keeps their kid for longer than their court-allotted time and the other parent calls the police.

This data is more than twenty years old. What is the real data in 2020?

421,394 missing-child incidents were reported to the FBI in 2019. And again, a large number of those kids are runaways - about 91%.

Here's what isn't true:

Mask-wearing is a scheme to kidnap children.

A globalist pedophile cabal is trafficking children - and only Trump can defeat it.

1 in 6 of runaway children are likely victims of sex trafficking.

Here's what is true...

Bodysnatchers aren't as common as you think.

Most children are abused by someone they trust

Trying to "save the children" can actually hurt those who are involuntarily in the commercial sex industry.

Consequences

Because the Federal Trafficking Victims Act of 2000 set the age of 18 as the dividing line for sale of sex, if a person is under 18, they're trafficked, even if no pimp is involved. So, if a teen running from a bad home situation trades a sexual act for a meal or a place to sleep at night, they are considered trafficked. The broad term "trafficking" abstracts the factors of poverty and displacement experienced by children.

These statistics are currently being used by far-right groups, including QAnon, in support of President Donald Trump. He signed an executive order earlier this year in effort to combat trafficking, turning over a $77 million budget to the Department of Justice.

As nonpartisan and partisan groups alike hunt for the statistics that will politicize their point, the social factors leading to child displacement go overlooked.
